Beyond the New Media Frame

Dot Tuer writes about Vera Frenkel's art thing called "String Games," where she played around with videos and tech stuff back in 1974 to tell stories about forgetting things and how images go around in our electronic world. Frenkel was doing something pretty new by mixing up live video chats between cities way before everyone was doing it on the internet. Tuer says Frenkel was kind of ahead of her time, doing cool things with screens and games that made people think about how we remember or lose memories with all this tech. But, it turns out, not many people really got how important this "String Games" was until much later, which Tuer thinks is because it was a bit too smart or different from the usual techy art stuff. The writing tries to show how Frenkel's old video game is a big deal for understanding the fancy art and memory stuff with computers and screens. Why do you think it took so long for people to appreciate Vera Frenkel's "String Games"?
